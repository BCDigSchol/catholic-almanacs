Project Summary

The project transforms a dense list of institution names, places, and people featured in nineteenth-century Catholic almanacs into a social history users can see. By digitizing and structuring the data found in these printed almanacs, users will be able both to see a macro view of Catholic institutions in the nineteenth century and dig into specific details about any given institution. Our current centerpiece is a map of all the institutions and how they change over time as new churches form and the boundaries of dioceses expand. Using our database, users can search for individuals and institutions to see what the almanacs say about them; eventually, we will also have those records to connect to other existing data about those people and organizations.

The Original Data Creation Process

In the Spring and Summer of 2024, a team of outstanding BC humanities students under the direction of Ashlyn Stewart and funded by the Libraries Catholic Collections Initiative undertook the crucial and unglamorous transcribing and encoding work required to launch the project. The students tagged (and, thus, structured) each institution’s name, place, and affiliates for the 1870 almanac in XML format. We then transformed the XML data into a .csv format to power our visualizations more easily. Some of our most important data encoding and structuring decisions are described on our Data Details Page.

Continuing Data Creation

Students are now working from both ends of the almanac run, extracting each institution’s name, place, and affiliates for almanacs in two periods: 1834-43 and 1864-69. Students compare the 1869 Almanac against our 1870 data, identifying places that have changed to create the 1869 dataset without having to encode from wholecloth. If there is no change year over year, the students are able to pretty quickly fill in a diocese's information based on the following year. However, if there are changes (which often happens with an individual's name or place name), encoding the data takes a bit longer. The process will continue year by year until the two teams meet in the 1850s. Then, the team will return to 1871 and move forward in the same fashion through 1895.

Our team adds new data about different dioceses in different years every day and publishes updated data quarterly. If you don't see a diocese in a particular year that you're curious about, drop us a line [ADD LINK HERE]; we can give you an estimate of when it will be ready.

Project Staff

Project Leadership

Ashlyn Stewart, PhD, founding co-director and project editor, is a digital scholarship specialist for the Boston College Libraries' Digital Scholarship Group. Ashlyn serves as project manager, trains and oversees a team of research assistants to extract and structure the almanacs data, and creates visualizations with the data.

Seth Meehan, PhD, founding co-director, is the associate director of academic and programming and special projects for the Boston College Libraries. Seth works with stakeholders across and beyond the university to identify opportunities for data from the almanacs to advance research.
