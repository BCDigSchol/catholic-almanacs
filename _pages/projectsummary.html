Project Summary

The project transforms a dense list of institution names, places, and people featured in nineteenth-century Catholic almanacs into a social history users can see. By digitizing and structuring the data found in these printed almanacs, users will be able both to see a macro view of Catholic institutions in the nineteenth century and dig into specific details about any given institution. Using our database, users can search for individuals and institutions to see what the almanacs say about them. Users can also explore our maps of all the institutions and dioceses to see how they change over time as new churches form and the boundaries of dioceses shift. Stay tuned for more visualizations of demographic data and connections to other existing data about Catholic leaders and organizations.
