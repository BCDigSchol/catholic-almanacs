Project Leadership

Ashlyn Stewart, PhD, founding co-director and project editor, is a digital scholarship speciaist for the Boston College Libraries' Digital Scholarship Group. Ashlyn serves as project manager, trains and oversees a team of research assistants to extract and structure the almanacs data, and creates visualizations with the data.

Seth Meehan, PhD, founding co-director, is the associate director of academic and programming and special projects for the Boston College Libraries. Seth works with stakeholders across and beyond the university to identify opportunities for data from the almanacs to advance reserach.

Current contributors

  • Kaitlyn Bell, founding reseach assistant (Spring 2024-) has encoded fifteen dioceses, from Cleveland to Galveston, from the 1870 Almanac. She has also identified thousands of place names and locations for defunct cities across scores of dioceses.

Past contributors

Listed in alphabetical order:

  • Christian Egan (Summer 2024) encoded a dozen dioceses, from San Francisco to Rochester, in the 1870 Almanac. He also identified hundreds of place names and locations for defunct cities across several of dioceses.
  • Sean Henniger (Summer 2024) matched town names from the 1870 almanac to current town names.
  • JeeHyun Lee (Summer 2024) identified hundreds of place names and locations of defunct towns listed in the 1870 almanac.
  • Elliott Jones (Spring-Summer 2024) encoded Oregon City and Albany in the 1870 almanac.
  • Victoria Oliviero, founding research assistant (Spring-Summer 2024), encoded twenty-two diocese, from Baltimore to Kansas, in the 1870 almanac. She also encoded institutions for several other dioceses and found place names for defuct cities.
  • Curran Schestag (Spring 2024) encoded Pittsburgh and Portland in the 1870 almanac.
  • Katerina Trantzas (Summer 2024) assigned custom unique IDs to 7000 individuals named in the 1870 almanac.
